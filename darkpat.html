Dark Pattern Directory

Welcome to our Dark Pattern Directory, where we highlight websites known for employing deceptive design tactics. Stay informed and protect yourself from manipulative practices by exploring the list below.

Amazon(Hidden Costs)

Amazon sometimes includes additional fees, such as shipping costs or taxes, during the checkout process, which may not be clearly disclosed upfront.

Booking.com(Urgency)

Booking.com often uses tactics such as displaying messages like "Only 1 room left at this price!" to create a sense of urgency and pressure users into making a booking decision quickly.

CNN(Disguised Ads)

CNN displays sponsored posts in users' newsfeeds, often blending seamlessly with organic content, making it difficult to distinguish between sponsored content and genuine posts.

GoDaddy(Roach Motel)

GoDaddy's domain registration process may include pre-selected options for additional services or auto-renewals, making it challenging for users to opt-out or cancel these services later.

Etsy(Forced Continuity)

Etsy offers free trials for its premium subscription services, but users may be automatically enrolled in a paid subscription after the trial period ends, without clear disclosure or easy cancellation options.

Instagram(Privacy Zuckering)

Instagram's privacy settings may be presented in a confusing or misleading manner, making it difficult for users to understand and control the visibility of their personal information and activity on the platform..
